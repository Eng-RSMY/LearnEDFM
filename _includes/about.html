LearnEDFM in a nutshell.

  • Naturally fractured rocks

    Much of the world’s water and energy resources are located in naturally fractured reservoirs within the earth’s crust. Understanding the dynamics of such reservoirs in terms of flow and transport is crucial to successful application of geothermal energy production in the future.

  • An novel numerical method

    Simulation of flow and transport through fractured porous media is challenging due to the high permeability contrast between the fractures and the surrounding rock matrix. The embedded discrete fracture model (EDFM), which treat fracture and matrix in two separate computational domains is a promising new technique in modelling.

  • Coupled flow and transport

    The governing equations of single phase flow and transport (including diffusion and advection) are discretized using a finite volume scheme. Separate computational domains are employed for fracture and matrix which leads to a coupled system of equations to be solved. An efficient coupling strategy is applied for numerical performance of the model.

  • Live results

    Thanks to the implementation in MATLAB, the simulations' results can be viewed directly and in real-time. A simple input file structure allows for easy modifications to adjust everything to your needs immediately!
